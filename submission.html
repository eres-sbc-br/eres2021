Topics of Interest

Topics of interest are related to practices of Open Science for Software Engineering research. They include, but are not limited to:

  • Open and FAIR data for software engineering
  • Trusted open repositories for software engineering
  • Data management plans for software engineering
  • Teaching and learning Open Science for software engineering
  • Open Science tools for software engineering
  • Interoperability of open data for software engineering
  • Open peer review practices for software engineering
  • Text and data mining for software engineering
  • Policies for open data in software engineering
  • Research open data management for software engineering
  • Responsible research and innovation for software engineering
  • Open science standards for software engineering
  • Provenance and governance of open science data for software engineering
  • Open science applied to software engineering empirical studies
  • Private data sharing solutions for software engineering
  • Data curation practices for software engineering

Submission Guidelines

  • OpenScienSE invites everyone to submit vision papers (3 pages) and experience reports or emerging results (up to 6 pages) written in English (preferable) or in Portuguese.
  • All papers must be submitted in PDF format and formatted according to the SBC template
  • Submitted papers must be unpublished and should not be under review elsewhere.
  • Acceptance of a paper requires at least one of its authors to be registered for the workshop and to present the paper.
  • The workshop proceedings will be published online at the SOL/SBC repository.
  • Papers should be submitted electronically through JEMS - https://submissoes.sbc.org.br/opensciense2021.

The Open Peer Reviewing Process

For this first edition of OpenScienSE, we will adopt the following open peer review model:

  • the reviewers will know who are the authors;
  • the authors will know who are the reviewers; and
  • all submitted papers, as well as their evaluation and discussion messages will be openly published in a public repository under Creative Commons 4.0 licenses at the end of the process.
